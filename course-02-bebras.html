Overview

The Bebras Challenge tests computational thinking — the ability to reason about problems the way a computer scientist does — without requiring any programming. Over a full term we built those mental tools from the ground up and rehearsed them against official Bebras-style questions under realistic timed conditions.

What students explored

  • Computational thinking — what it is and why it matters far beyond computing.
  • Algorithms & logic — step-by-step reasoning, rules and conditions, following and designing procedures.
  • Pattern recognition & abstraction — spotting structure and stripping a problem to its essentials.
  • Decomposition — breaking a hard problem into solvable pieces.
  • Data representation — encodings, sequences and simple structures.
  • Spreadsheets as a thinking tool — a practical demo of organising and reasoning about data.
  • Timed practice — official practice quizzes to build speed and accuracy before the real challenge.

Skills developed

  • Logical reasoning and structured problem-solving.
  • Abstraction and decomposition — the core of computer science.
  • Composure and accuracy under time pressure.
