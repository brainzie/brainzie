Science & Electromagnetic Waves

Our very first enrichment cohort — an introduction to the physics of waves and the electromagnetic spectrum, ending with a hands-on Morse-code signalling lab.

Overview

The pilot edition of STEMming for you set the tone for everything that followed: take a genuinely interesting slice of science, treat students as capable thinkers, and make it tangible. Over six weekly sessions we explored how waves carry energy and information all around us — and then put the theory to work by sending and decoding real signals.

What students explored

  • The nature of waves — amplitude, frequency and wavelength, and what those properties actually mean.
  • The electromagnetic spectrum — from radio waves to gamma rays, and the everyday technology that sits on each band.
  • How information rides on a signal — the leap from "a wave" to "a message".
  • Encoding & Morse code — representing letters as patterns of short and long pulses.
  • A decoding lab — students worked with real Morse-code audio recordings, decoding messages by ear at different speeds.
Hands-on highlight: the course closed with a Morse-code signalling and decoding activity using purpose-made audio — turning an abstract idea about waves into a message students could actually crack.

Skills developed

  • Scientific vocabulary and the confidence to use it precisely.
  • Connecting physics to the technology students use every day.
  • Pattern recognition, encoding and careful listening.
