Overview

The Junior Mathematical Challenge rewards clever thinking over rote calculation. Across eleven weekly sessions we built a toolkit of problem-solving tactics and practised them against genuine past papers — including the JMC (2023 and 2024) and the follow-on Kangaroo round — so that on the day the format held no surprises.

What students explored

  • Number agility — fast, flexible arithmetic and estimation.
  • Logic & puzzles — reasoning carefully from given facts.
  • Geometry & spatial reasoning — seeing the shape of a problem.
  • Tactics — working backwards, eliminating options, spotting symmetry and smart estimation.
  • Exam technique — multiple-choice strategy, pacing and when to move on.
  • Real past papers — JMC 2023, JMC 2024 and Kangaroo 2024 worked through together.

Skills developed

  • Speed and accuracy under exam conditions.
  • A repertoire of problem-solving strategies, not just formulae.
  • Calm, methodical thinking when a problem looks unfamiliar.
