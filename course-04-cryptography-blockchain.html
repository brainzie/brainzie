Technology: Cryptography & Blockchain

How the digital world keeps secrets and decides what to trust — from pen-and-paper ciphers to the ideas behind blockchain.

Overview

Every time you log in, pay online or send a message, cryptography is working invisibly in the background. This edition pulled back the curtain — starting with ciphers students could break by hand, then building up to the modern mathematics of encryption, and finishing with an honest look at blockchain: what it genuinely solves, and where the hype outruns reality.

What students explored

  • Classical ciphers — Caesar and substitution ciphers, encrypting and decrypting by hand.
  • Code-breaking — frequency analysis and why "secret" codes fall.
  • Keys & modern encryption — the idea of symmetric keys and the clever trick of public-key cryptography.
  • Hashing — digital fingerprints, and why you can't run them backwards.
  • Digital signatures — proving who sent something and that it wasn't changed.
  • Blockchain — chained hashes, distributed ledgers and consensus — what they're good for, and what they're not.

Skills developed

  • Security thinking — assuming an adversary and reasoning about trust.
  • Comfort with abstraction and a little applied mathematics.
  • Healthy scepticism about technology claims, and the ethics behind them.
